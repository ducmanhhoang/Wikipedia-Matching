0Design and developmentThe G.12 was an all-metal low-wing cantilever personnel transport aircraft.
1It had three radial engines, one mounted on the fuselage nose and the other two in wing-mounted nacelles.
2The engines drove three-blade feathering metal propellers.
3The mainwheels of its landing gear retracted into the nacelles; the tailwheel was fixed.
4The flight deck and cabin were fully enclosed.
5Access was via a port-side access door aft of the wing.The G.12 was designed as a civil aircraft, but served mainly in military roles during the war.
6Only a limited number were built, some as late as 1944, after the Italian armistice.
7The G.12 inspired the postwar G.212 "Flying Classroom", the last Italian three-engine transporter.
