0Giuseppe Merosi (8 December 1872 – 27 March 1956) was a famous Italian automobile engineer.Born in Piacenza, Merosi trained as a building surveyor, before he discovered his gift for the automotive engineering.
1He gained first experiences at Bianchi and the running department of Fiats.
2Soon he was hired (1910) as a new chief engineer to new company A.L.F.A which was established at Portello in Milan.
3The predecessor company, which had manufactured licensed products of the French automaker Darracq, had failed.
4The first Alfa designed by Merosi was A.L.F.A 24 HP, which came on the market on 24 June 1910.
5From its 4.1 litres capacity were obtained a proud 42 hp and the cars reached , at that time a very considerable maximum speed.
6In the first year already 50 pieces were sold.
7The cars proved to be durable and reliable vehicles with an elegant design.
8Higher HP models followed in the coming years.
9In 1914 Merosi designed the first Alfa Romeo DOHC engine, 4 cylinders, 4.5 litres and 16 valve head, this was used in 1914 Alfa Romeo Grand Prix car.
10World War I stopped development and it wasn't until 1922 Merosi came again with DOHC engine.
11The beginning of the 20's also saw the luxurious Alfa Romeo G1.Merosi recognized also the meaning of racing both for the development and for the mark image.
12Thus already 1911 Alfa Romeo HP took part in the Targa Florio.
13The first pure racing cars were starting from 1920 the RL and RM as well as the P1.
141923 Targa Florio was won for the first time.1926 Merosi left the company and he was replaced with new chief engineer Vittorio Jano.
15He worked afterwards for Isotta Fraschini and died at the age of 84 in 1956.
