0Born in Piacenza was one of the greatest Italian designers of cars.
1After studying, surveyor, and military service opened in 1893 as a bicycle factory, active until 1898.
2He was then taken to the Orio and Marchand, the company for which he designed cars and motorcycles until the summer of 1904.
3He then went to the Technical Office of FIAT, working on the project of some of the competition cars.
4At the end of 1905 he moved to Milan, making for the Lentz, the project of a car which was then made in three copies.
5In the autumn of 1906 he was employed by the Whites for whom he worked until September 1909.
6In 1910 he became the technical director and head of the office for projects of the new plant in Portello Milan of what at the time was the newly formed ALFA, Anonima Lombarda Fabbrica Automobili.
7The first car designed was the ALFA 24 HP, which was distributed from June 24, 1910, and had a displacement of 4,100 cc with a power of 42 hp, which was getting the car to 100 km/h speed.
8The first year of the model, robust and with a nice line, sold 50 copies.
9Followed projects of other cars more powerful.In 1914 Merosi designed the first engine that had a distribution system with twin-cam 16-valves (8 for intake and 8 for exhaust), with 4 cylinders, 4.500 cc engine; this engine equipped to a vehicle explicitly for the competition.
10The activity of Merosi continued during the first world war with the construction of generator sets and compressors for the military and a move (on behalf of Nicola Romeo) at the Officine Ferroviarie Meridionali during the period 1917-1918, going back to the civil design in 1919, for the same company which in the meantime had changed his name in what today is known as the Alfa Romeo, with the design of another twin cam engine.
11At the beginning of the twenties came out of the luxurious Alfa Romeo G1.Many recognized also the importance of racing to the development and the image of the company.
12In 1911 the ALPHA he participated for the first time at the Targa Florio with the 24HP while the first win at this historic race is dated 1923.
13The later cars, these real racing cars were the Alfa RL, the Alpha ROM and the Alpha P1.In April 1926 Giuseppe Merosi left the company and was replaced by Vittorio Jano.
14He worked later for other companies in the branch including the Isotta Fraschini, returning to Alfa Romeo during the second world war between 1941 and 1943.
15It is off at the age of 84 in Lecco.
