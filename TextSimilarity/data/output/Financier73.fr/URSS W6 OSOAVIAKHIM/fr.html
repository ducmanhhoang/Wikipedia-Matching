0The W6 OSOAVIAKHIM is an airship semi-rigid built in the USSR and designed by the aeronautical engineer Italian Umberto Nobile.
1The name of the aircraft was borrowed from the name of the establishment where it was manufactured : "the Society for the promotion of defence, aviation and chemistry" (in Russian ОСОАВИАХИМ).
2The airship W6 was the biggest airship ever built in the Soviet Union, and this will be the one that will have the greatest success.
3This aircraft has held for 20 years the record of length of time in continuous flight since October 1937 with 130 hours and 27 minutes by the commander Ivan Pankow.
4Features techniquesTrès little information has been published on the aircraft, as in all productions the soviets.
5The only elements known are those of the archives of Umberto Nobile, the designer of which, although communist, posted, went about teaching the technique of airships in the United States and returned in Italy in 1943.
6HistoireA the end of the 1920s, the young Soviet Union" is very interested in the exploits of the great airships such as the Nobile N. 1 Norge and LZ 127 Graf Zeppelin.
7The leaders wanted to develop their own national program but has none of the engineering specialist in this field and wanting to advance very quickly in their program, have a guest in the USSR, the Italian Umberto Nobile, recognized at the time as the best specialist of the airships.
8The engineer had been dishonoured, in the eyes of fascism, after the disaster of the airship Nobile N. 2 Italia when returning from his 3rd expedition to the North Pole in 1928.
9In addition, the Italian investments in the airships had been seriously reduced.
10The Russian proposal was the perfect opportunity for Nobile to improve its image and to the leader fascist who could temporarily exclude a character annoying for its air force.
11Also a possible success of Nobile abroad could also be useful for propaganda purposes.Umberto Nobile comes to Moscow in may 1932.
12At the beginning, he must face several difficulties : the aircraft factory was nothing more than a simple hangar for dirigibles-free workshops and tooling mechanics.
13Nobile has in advance ensure the training of crews and technicians for the construction of new airships.
14It was not until the summer of 1933 it was possible to begin the construction of the airship W6, aircraft in all points similar to the Nobile N. 1 Norge and Nobile N. 2 Italia.These difficulties seem to have stemmed from the fact that in the Soviet Union, never the direction and responsibility of projects of such importance have been entrusted to a stranger.
15The soviet engineers had built earlier airship-semi-rigid 9150 m3, which was completed in August 1934 W7 receive the designation.
16The airship was destroyed a few days after the end of a fire shed.
17(According to another source the W7 airship was a joint project of Nobile, Felice Trojani, and Katanski, and had a volume of 38 000 m3 The exploitationPendant the duration of his stay in the Soviet Union from 1931 to 1936, Umberto Nobile designed airships for the transportation of passengers.
18The W6 was to ensure the regular line between Moscow and Sverdlovsk.
19It could not be used because there was no post boarding, no mast anchor or the possibility of refuelling.
20In 1936, a mast mooring has finally been erected in Sverdlovsk.In 1937, captain Pankow led the W6 during 79 hours on the route Moscow-Sverdlovsk-Moscow with the continuous operation of the engines.
21This was the record of operation of 130 hours and 27 minutes, which was not defeated until 20 years later.
22The race record began in Dolgoproudny (Russian Долгопрудный) on 29 September and ended on 4 October.
23The distance was about 5,000 km.
24This trip was the preparation of a regular link in the airship.
25The catastropheLe February 5, 1938, at 16h45, a group of explorers and researchers soviets, led by Ivan Papanin, was surprised by sudden movements of the drifting ice in the Arctic.
26The mission that was assigned to the crew of the W6 was to bring relief to these explorers and lead in Greenland.
27The expedition is part of the city of Murmansk.
28The journey between Moscow and Murmansk should allow to check the good behavior of the aircraft in arctic climate conditions.
29During this trip, the airship collided with the "Mont Neblo" (in Russian Небло), near the town of Kandalaksha, mount peaking at only 440 m altitude.
30According to the official version, the data contained on the card-board mentioned in the obstacle to only 30 m above sea level.
31The survivors would have however reported that the Commander Nikolai Gudowanzew was well wary of the data from the previous maps and, because of the poor visibility ambient, he had given the order to sail to 800 meters of altitude, but Ivan Pankow, the second in command, had put too much time before retransmitting this order, and the balloon stayed at an altitude of 300 m.
