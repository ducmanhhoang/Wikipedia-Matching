0Goeric of Metz (died September 19, 643), also known as Abbo I of Metz, Goericus of Metz, and Gury of Metz, was a bishop of Metz.
1BiographyHe was a married man with two daughters.
2He recovered his eyesight at St. Stephen's in Metz.
3Shortly thereafter, he joined the clergy and was ordained a priest by Arnulf of Metz.
4In 627, he succeeded Arnulf as bishop of Metz.
5As bishop, he transferred the relics of his predecessor Arnulf to the Church of the Apostles.
6He also built the church of Great St. Peter's and the monastery at Epinal for his two daughters, Precia and Victorina.
7He was also a personal friend of Dagobert I.
8He died in 643.
9He has the feast day of September 19.
10In the 10th century, his relics were brought from Saint-Symphorien to Epinal.
11This event is commemorated in the local Calendar of Saints on April 15.
