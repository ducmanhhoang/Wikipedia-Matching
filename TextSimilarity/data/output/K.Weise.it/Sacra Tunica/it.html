11Rock Originally the Sacred Tunic was mentioned for the first time on may 1, 1196, when archbishop of Trier, John, consecrated the main altar of the then newly erected in the choir of the Trier Cathedral and took in the relics.
12When the emperor Maximilian I came to Trier on the occasion of the meeting of the parliament of the kingdom of 1512, he asked to see the Holy Tunic.
13Archbishop Richard von Greiffenklau zu Vollrads he, like many other bishops and prelates in the presence of the emperor, open the altar.
14After the divine Office, celebrated in memory of the deceased wife of Maximilian I, the citizens asked in a loud voice to the exposure of the tunic.
15The Chapter of the Cathedral he had built, so say the woodcuts of the era, a balcony on the west side of the apse, from which, as from 30 June took place numerous performances.Up to 1517 there were, annually, pilgrimages to the Holy Tunic in Trier.
16At the suggestion of pope Leo X, the pilgrimages were to be held subsequently in accordance with the trips to the shrine of Aachen.
17So the subsequent performances were established every seven years: 1524; 1531; 1538 and 1545.
18Because of the disorders consequent on the reformation and the wars, the cadence of the exposures was abandoned, and finally ceased.
19The Sacred Tunic was kept, from 1632 to 1794, except for a few break in the Fortress of Ehrenbreitstein in Koblenz.
