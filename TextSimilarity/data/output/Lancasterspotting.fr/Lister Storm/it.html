0The Lister Storm is a car racing (approved) for use on the road, built by the English Lister Cars in 1993 in the four specimens before to decide on the suspension of the manufacture.
1The engine in question is a 7.0 L (6996cm3) lender 546 HP and 790 Nm of torque, based on the one used by the Jaguar XJR-12 competition the team played in the 24 Hours of Le Mans.The car is not equipped with antilock brakes, but incorporates front brakes Brembo 14-inch rear 12.5 that serve the same function.The traction control is produced by Racelogic.The grip is guaranteed by the frame model of 'ground effect' for channelling the air flow under the car, thus creating a downward force.The suspensions are double wishbones with stabilizer bars at the front and at the rear axle.The bodywork is monocoque in aluminium honeycomb, on which you insert the sunroof and outer panels of carbon.The Storm is equipped with the front engine/rear wheel drive (FR) weighs 1664 kg, capable of a 0-100 in 4.1 seconds.
2Today, the Lister produces only prototypes from the race.
3The Lister Storm GTS initially debuted at the 24 Hours of Le Mans in 1995 in the GT1 class in the face of the passenger car of the calibre of McLaren F1, Ferrari F40, Jaguar XJ220 and Porsche 911 GT2.
4The car had the colors of the Newcastle United football team, which sponsored the team car.
5The car driven by Geoff Lees and Rupert Keegan, unfortunately, not all went well in the race, because of a fault in the gearbox after just 40 laps.In 1996, the team decided to do a pre-test in view of the participation in the race on the Circuit de la Sarthe participating in the 24 Hours of Daytona.
6Unfortunately, the Storm failed here.
7Even after this defeat, the team decided to compete at Le Mans with the Storm GTS, who managed to finish the race, but in the nineteenth position and to 59 laps from the winner.
8Following this series of events is anything but positive, the vertices of the Lister decided to register the Lister GTS the BPR Global GT Series debut at the fifth race at the Nurburgring.
9The car did not succeed, however, never to conclude a race.
