0The Macchi M. 39 was a idrocorsa (hydroplane racing) configuration with "boots", single-engine, and monoplane low-wing developed by the company the Italian air force, Aeronautica Macchi to participate in the Cup Schneider, 1926.
1The history of the progettoIn following the victory in the edition of 1925 of the Cup, Schneider, held in Baltimore, of the american James Doolittle at the controls of the Curtiss R3C-2, and in the light of the performance offered by the idrocorsa the british Gloster III and the Supermarine S. 4, all configuration boots to difference of the Italian Macchi M. 33 to the main hull, the Ministry of Aviation issued a request, dated February 4, 1926, for a new model that was equipped with the new Fiat engine AS.2 (Air Pushed), a 12-cylinder V, capable of delivering a power output of 850 HP (625 kW), built on project of the engineer Tranquillo Zerbi.
2In order to meet this requirement, the technical office of the Aeronautica Macchi, directed by the engineer Mario Castoldi, decided to leave the setting to the main hull wing biplane, only in the M. 33 monoplana, until then, used in the making of their idrocorsa, to focus on the configuration that has already been adopted by the competition, a seaplane with a fuselage of conventional, combined with a structure of double-float and single-storey wing with a thin profile, positioned low strengthened by thin rods of V brace upside down.
3Satisfied with the preliminary projects, the ministry issued an order for the supply of five specimens, two intended for training of pilots, characterized by a wingspan of 10,23 m and from the different group motoelica, a Fiat A. 22 from 550/600 HP coupled to a propeller wooden two, identified with the military records M. M. 72 and 73, and the other three, the M. M. 74, 75 and 76, in the configuration of stroke, equipped with the engine AS.2 equipped with two-blade propeller with metal and with a wingspan reduced to 9,26 m. TecnicaCome and whereas, the M. 39 was a idrocorsa from the setting, to the era of the modern, innovative in the panorama of the Italian sports sector, construction of mixed wood and metal, cell monoplana with group motoelica in tractor configuration and the floating "boots".
4The fuselage was made with a structure entirely made of wood, covered with plywood, and only in the sections near the engine, metal.
5The wing uses the same principle: two frame members molded case circuit breakers in the fir, on which were placed the ribs pulleys supporting a plywood covering.
6The wing was reinforced with a series of steel tie rods high-strength, that connected the boots with the longitudinal members and the latter with the firewall.
7The feature that distinguished this aircraft from earlier was surely the presence of the radiators to touch on the back and the belly of the wings and on the front of the muzzle.
8These were made of brass tubes with reduced thickness, inside of which flowed through the cooling liquid.
9The building of the "spartan" of the plane, testified his vocation velocistica, also underlined by the research of the maximum lightness.
10ImpiegoIl the first M. 39 to detach from the surface of the Lake of Varese, in front of the seaplane base of the Schiranna owned by Macchi, was the M. M. 72 training, on July 6, 1926 .
11, while the first exemplary competition flew the next 6 September, when the cycle test was started from the 30th of August with one of the two exemplars from the training to the commands Romeo Sartori.
12Even reaching the speed of 414 km/h in the test on the 17th of September, from the evidence emerged from a series of drawbacks, both in flight, in particular, emerged problems of manoeuvrability, which in the technical part, because it found difficulty in the carburation of the engine AS.2; in this second case, they tried to overcome the defects in the site, also using the experience of the test pilot Mario de Bernardi.
13A few days later, on the 21st of September, on the 16th according to different sources, because of an unexpected aerodynamic stall, the captain Vittorio Centurione Scotto, at the controls of one of the two exemplars from training, lost control of the aircraft remaining killed in the accident.On the 13th of November 1926, the three M. 39 in the configuration of the race took part in the edition of 1926 of the Coppa Schneider, the circuit of Hampton Roads, near Norfolk, Virginia, United States of America.
