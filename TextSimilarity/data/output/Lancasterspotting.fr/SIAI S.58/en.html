0The SIAI S.58 or Savoia-Marchetti S.M.58 was an Italian flying boat fighter prototype of the 1920s designed and manufactured by SIAI.
1S.58In 1924, the Regia Marina (Italian Royal Navy) issued a requirement for a replacement for its Macchi M.7ter flying boat fighter.
2To compete with the Macchi M.26 for a production order as the replacement, SIAI developed the S.58.
3It was a wooden, single-seat, single-bay biplane with a hull based on that of the SIAI S.51 racing flying boat which had participated in the 1922 Schneider Trophy race.
4The S.58s engine, a 221-kilowatt (296-brake horsepower) Hispano-Suiza HS 42 V8 driving a pusher propeller, was mounted on struts above the hull.
5SIAI proposed an armament of two fixed, forward-firing 7.7-millimeter (0.303-inch) Vickers machine guns mounted on the hull on either side of the cockpit.Green and Swanborough, p. 527.
6The S.58 made its first flight early in the summer of 1924 and on 25 August 1924 set a world altitude record for aircraft of its type by reaching 5,831 meters (19,130 feet) with a payload of 250 kilograms (551 pounds).
7SIAI built three prototypes, but the Regia Marina opted to save money by re-engining the Macchi M.7ter to extend its service life rather than purchase a new aircraft.
8SIAI did, however, build at least one more S.58, which the Scuola di Velocit√† (High Speed School) at Desenzano used.Green and Swanborough, p. 527.
9SIAI later fitted the first S.58 prototype with a 313-kilowatt (420-horsepower) Fiat A.20 V12, redesignating the re-engined aircraft the S.58bis.
10In 1927, the Regia Marina again held a contest for a replacement for the Macchi M.7ter, and SIAI entered the S.58bis.
11The Regia Marina found it impressive enough to make plans to procure 97 S.58bis aircraft, but these plans were cancelled when the Regia Marina again chose as an economy measure to extend the M.7ter{{'}}s service life by re-engining it, and no production order materialized for the S.58bis.
12Green and Swanborough, p. 527.
13S.58terIn the autumn of 1928, the S.58ter, a slightly modified S.58bis also employing the Fiat A 20 V12 engine, flew for the first time.
14In 1929, the Regia Marina again organized a contest for an M.7ter replacement, and SIAI entered the S.58ter.
15The S.58ter lost out to the Macchi M.41bis and did not win a production order.Green and Swanborough, p. 527.
