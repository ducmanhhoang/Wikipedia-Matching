0The Savoia-Marchetti S.56 was an Italian single-engine biplane flying boat trainer and tourer, built by Savoia-Marchetti.
1Of unequal span, the upper longer than the lower,Donald 1997, p.820.
2of wooden construction.
3Instructor and student sat side by side in separate cockpits, with dual controls; a third cockpit was located just aft that.
4It used a 52 kW (70 hp) Anzani.The S.56A had a slightly longer span and 60 kW (80 hp) Anzani, and turned into amphibians by addition of manually retractable landing gear.
5Private owners and flying clubs purchased at least 12, and Regia Aeronautica operated four (differing in engine specification, including 96 kW {115 hp} FIAT A.53, 101 kW {135 hp} FIAT A.54, and Walter Venus radial engines, among others).
6The S.56A proved popular in the U.S., and the American Aeronautical Corporation (AAC) set up licence production in 1929, relying on the 67 kW (90 hp) Kinner K5 radials for power for three two-place aircraft and over 40 three-seaters.
7This was followed in 1930 by the S.56B, powered by the 93 kW (125 hp) Kinner B-5, with an enclosed cockpit.
8Another was converted to a single-seater and given additional fuel tanks, as the S.56C, for an attempted round-the-world flight by American businessman Smith Reynolds.In 1932, the Budd Company built an all-metal S.56 as the Budd BB-1 Pioneer.
