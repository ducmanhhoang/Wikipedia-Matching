0SviluppoProgettato by engineer Alessandro Marchetti, for participation in the Coppa Schneider, the S. 65 was given for testing at the pilot Tommaso Dal Molin, only in July 1929; with only 40 days before the start of the competition, the aircraft was to be discarded even if the first results were encouraging.In the month of January of 1930 the aircraft was to be modified in the floating and in the wing, saving significant weight.On the lake of Garda, on the 18th of January 1930, in the takeoff, following a sharp detachment from the surface of the water, Dal Molin was not able to correct the trim of the aircraft that sank, causing the death of the pilot.From the findings carried out on the wreck of the aircraft, it was established that the accident was caused by the deformation of one of the struts that triggered the tail.
