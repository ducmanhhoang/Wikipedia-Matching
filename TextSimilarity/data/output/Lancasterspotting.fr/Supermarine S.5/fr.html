0The Supermarine S. 5 seaplane race single-engine and single-seater racing of the 1920s, built by Supermarine.
1Designed specifically for the Trophy Schneider, the S. 5 was the progenitor of a lineage of racing planes that ultimately led to the Supermarine Spitfire.
2Design and développementLe Supermarine S. 5 was designed by Reginald Mitchell for the Trophy Schneider 1927.
3Following the loss of the S. 4 just before the edition 1925 the Trophy Schneider, Mitchell designed a new monoplane racing metal.
4Unlike S. 4, and its structure is entirely wood, the S. 5 is constructed mainly of duralumin.
5The aircraft was equipped with a low-wing tethered with spars and ribs of spruce covered with a plywood sheathing.
6Three aircraft were built, one with an engine with direct-drive Napier Lion VIIA of 900 hp and the other two with an engine Napier Lion VIIB of 875 hp equipped with gear motor.Lumsden and Heffernan 1989, p. 290.
7Operational history the first aircraft flew for the first time on June 7, 1927.
8The S. 5-types finished 1st and 2nd in the Cup Schneider 1927, which was held in Venice, the aeroplane winner (Serial number N220) was flown by Flight Lieutenant S. N Webster, who flew at an average speed of 281.66 mph (453.28 km/h).
9Andrews and Morgan 1987, p. 186.
10N221 crashed during a record attempt speed, the 12 March 1928, killing Flight Lieutenant Samuel Kinkead who flew it (he had flown the Gloster IV Trophy Schneider 1927).
11Andrews and Morgan 1987, p. 186.
12The engine Napier has reached its limits in terms of performance, Mitchell redessina the plane for the Trophy Schneider 1929 in the equipping of the new engine Rolls-Royce R of the Supermarine S. 6.
13Worried about the lack of reliability of the Gloster VI, the High Speed Flight decided to commit a S. 5 (N219) as well as two S. 6 for the race.
14The S. 5 flown by Flight Lieutenant D'arcy r.a. greig finished the race in third position in 46 minutes 15 seconds at a speed of 282.11 mph (454.20 km/h), behind the S. 6 winner piloted by Flying Officer H. R. Waghorn and a Macchi M. 52.
15Andrews and Morgan 1987, pp. 192-193.
16RépliqueRay Hilborne designed and built a replica to scale 1 of S. 5, which flew for the first time on 28 August 1975.
17The replica, powered by a Continental engine IO-360, was built of wood and had received some modifications: the wing was modified to lower the stall speed, the rudders were installed, the cockpit was enlarged and the mass was reduced to 680 kg (less than half of that of the S. 5 original).
18Hall, 1976, p. 576.
