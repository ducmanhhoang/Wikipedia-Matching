0The acanthus with soft leaves (Acanthus mollis) is a plant of the family of Acanthacées, also called acanthus soft or acanthus leaf large.
1It is known for its leaves, used for architectural design, for example, on the corinthian capitals.
2This species is very frequent on the shores of the mediterranean.
3Device végétatifL'acanthus soft is a plant of 30 cm to 80 cm in height, but may reach 1.50 m, inflorescence included.
4Its numerous basal leaves have a long petiole, the set can reach nearly a meter.
5They are large (30 to 60 cm long), opposite, deep green, shiny, soft to the touch.
6These leaves are large, divided into several lobes, toothed but not spiny (as opposed to Acanthus spinosus).
7Device reproducteurLa flowering occurs in this species between may and August.
8The inflorescence is a long spike cylindrical, in fact, a cyme uniparous helicoid of 30 to 40 cm, many-flowered, with bisexual.
9Each flower is surrounded by three bracts, bract with central cut-out and thorny and larger in size than the other two.
10The calyx has two lips, of which the upper is pretty long and forming a sort of "helmet" at the top of the corolla is often tinged purple on the top.
11The corolla is reduced to a lower lip white, veined with purple-pink, three-lobed pendants ; the flower can reach 5 cm long .
12The 4 stamens are fused to the corolla and look like tiny brushes to habit.
13The ovary is superior, it contains two carpels.
14Pollination is entomogame.
15The flower is set up in such a way that only bees or bumble bees large enough to force the passage between the sepal upper and lower lip can reach the nectar at the bottom of the tube.The fruit is a capsule loculicide of an ovoid shape, containing two to four large, black seeds at maturity.
