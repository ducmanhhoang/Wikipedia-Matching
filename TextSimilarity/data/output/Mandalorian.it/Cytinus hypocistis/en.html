0Cytinus hypocistis is an ant-pollinated species of parasitic plant in the family Cytinaceae having four subspecies.
1It is found primarily in locations that surround the Mediterranean Sea, and is the type for the genus Cytinus.
2The binomial has been conserved.
3Cytinus hypocistis is native to Albania; Algeria; Crete; Croatia; Cyprus; Greece; France (including Corsica); Israel; Italy (including Sardinia and Sicily); Lebanon; Libya; Malta; Morocco; Portugal; Spain (including both the Balearic and Canary Islands); Syria; Tunisia; and Turkey.The subspecies macranthus is native to Portugal and western Spain; orientalis is native to southern Greece and Crete; and pityusensis is endemic to Ibiza of the Balearic Islands.
4UsesCytinus hypocistis has been used in traditional medicine to treat dysentery and tumors of the throat, and has been used for its astringent qualities.
