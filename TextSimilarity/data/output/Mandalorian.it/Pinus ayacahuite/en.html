0Pinus ayacahuite, also called ayacahuite pine and Mexican white pine, (family Pinaceae) is a species of pine native to the mountains of southern Mexico and western Central America, in the Sierra Madre del Sur and the eastern end of the Eje Volcánico Transversal, between 14° and 21°N latitude in the Mexican states of Guerrero, Oaxaca, Puebla, Veracruz and Chiapas, and in Guatemala, El Salvador and Honduras.
1It grows on relatively moist areas with summer rainfalls, however specimens from its eastern and southern distribution live under really wet conditions; it needs full sun and well drained soils.
2Its temperature needs fluctuate between 19 to 10°C on average a year.
3This tree accepts from subtropical to cool climate.Pinus ayacahuite is a large tree, regularly growing to 30–45 m and exceptionally up to 50 m tall.
4It is a member of the white pine group, Pinus subgenus Strobus, and like all members of that group, the leaves ('needles') are in fascicles (bundles) of five, with a deciduous sheath.
5The needles are finely serrated, and 9–16 cm long.
6The cones are long and slender, 15–40 cm long and 4–6 cm broad (closed), opening to 6–10 cm broad; the scales are thin and flexible.
7The seeds are small, 6–8 mm long, and have a long slender wing 18–25 mm long.It is moderately susceptible to white pine blister rust (Cronartium ribicola), but in cultivation has proved somewhat less susceptible than most other American white pines (see e.g. western white pine, sugar pine).
8Cultivation and usesIt has been planted with ornamental and commercial purposes for paper industry at different latitudes and altitudes in different parts of the world: In temperate subtropical regions- Commercial production: It is planted on the highlands of South Africa.
9On the highest elevations of Southern Brazil.

11In temperate regions at middle and low altitudes: Argentine provinces of Salta, Tucuman and Cordoba; at 500 to 1500 m above sea level.

15It is grown as an ornamental tree in parks for its attractive foliage and very long cones, among the longest that can be grown in many temperate areas.The wood is valuable, used for general indoor construction work.
