0Pine, Jeffrey (Pinus jeffreyi Murray) is a tree of the family Pinaceae, native to north America.
1It takes its name from the botanist scotland John Jeffrey, active around the mid-NINETEENTH century.
2FoglieLe leaves are needle-like, long about 25 cm, rigid, blue-green, gathered in groups of three and taken to the branches usually smooth.
3FioriI male flowers are red, which becomes yellow at the time of the opening; the female flowers are red-purple on the separate clusters on the young branches at the beginning of the summer.
4Distribution and habitatIl pine Jeffrey is widespread in the western United States, mainly on the slopes of the Sierra Nevada in California, but also Oregon's southwest and in northern Mexico (Baja California).
