0The CANT Z. 1007 Alcione was a plane trimoteur medium bomber to a low wing, developed by the Italian company Cantieri Riuniti dell'adriatico - CRDA in the second half of the 1930s.
1This aircraft bombardier has, thanks to its versatility, was used in the missions of aerial reconnaissance by the Regia Aeronautica and the Luftwaffe.
2During the second world war it was widely used on the front Greek, at Malta, and during the war in England.
3DéveloppementLes studies began in 1935 under the responsibility of the aerospace engineer Italian Filippo Zappata recognized for the lines extremely clean and slender for his achievements.
4The principle of powerplant chosen was the trimoteur, typical solution devices italians of that class at the time.
5The constructor of Trieste presents the prototype in march 1937.
6The first prototype was equipped with engines, Isotta Fraschini, engine 12 cylinder V 60°, water-cooled power developing 825 Hp each, driving propellers bipales.
7The Regia Aeronautica passed an order of 34 aircraft, but equipped with bladed propellers with variable pitch.The engine Isotta Fraschini Asso XI of the first series do not give complete satisfaction, the manufacturer opted for the engine Piaggio P. XI R2C 40 14-cylinder star more powerful.

14The CANT Z. 1007bis was used as a bomber during the war italo-Greek, in the Aegean Sea in 1941, with which the 210ª Squadron Italian, sank the destroyer HMS Juno (F46) of the Royal Navy.
