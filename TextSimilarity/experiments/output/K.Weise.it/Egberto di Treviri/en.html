0Egbert (ca. 950 – 9 December 993) was the Archbishop of Trier from 977 until his death.
1Egbert was a son of Dirk II, Count of Holland.
2After being trained in the abbey of Egmond and the court of Bruno I, Archbishop of Cologne, he became the chancellor of Otto II in 976.
3The following year he was appointed to the archdiocese of Trier.
4In 984, he joined the conspiracy of Henry II, Duke of Bavaria, to dethrone Otto, but the following year returned to the allegiance of Otto.
5Egbert was a known patron of science and the arts.
6Egbert himself commissioned the Registrum Gregorii.
7He was also the recipient of the early Romanesque illustrated Codex Egberti, which was probably produced at Reichenau.
8Beginning with his tenure, however, Trier came to rival Reichenau as the artistic centre of the Ottonian world.
