0Reineldis (also Reinhild, Reinaldes, Rainelde among others; c. 630 – c. 700) was a saint of the 7th century, martyred by the Huns.
1LifeReineldis was born in a place called Condacum (which is identified with either Condé-sur-l'Escaut or Kontich).
2She was the daughter of Duke Witger of Lotharingia and Saint Amalberga of Maubeuge.
3Her brother Emebert was a priest in the diocese of Cambrai.
4Her biography mentions Gudula as her sister.
5Her mother entered the religious life at Maubeuge Abbey.
6Reineldis made a pilgrimage to the Holy Land.
7Her vita, written between 1048–1051 in Lobbes Abbey,Vita Reineldis, Acta Sanctorum, Julii IV, 173-178.
8records this fact, stating that she visited Jerusalem.She returned home and devoted herself to a life of charitable work at Saintes.
9She was decapitated by the Huns at Saintes, together with deacon Grimoaldus and her servant Gondulphus.
10VenerationSaint Reineldis is primarily venerated in Saintes as the patron saint of the town.
11Some sources even indicate that Saintes owes its name to Reineldis' martyrdom.
12The parish church of Saintes is dedicated to Sainte-Renelde since the Middle Ages and has preserved the relics of Saint Reineldis.
13This church has a large bell tower built in the 17th century.
14Saint Reineldis' patronage for eye diseases is due to the association with a water well in Saintes known as "Sainte Renelde's well", water which is believed to cure eye diseases.
15IconographyReineldis is commonly depicted with a sword or being dragged by her hair, referring to the decapitation.
16She is also portrayed as a pilgrim, because of her journey to the Holy Land.
