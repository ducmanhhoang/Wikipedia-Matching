0Design and developmentThe S.65 was a single-seat twin-engine floatplane of low-wing monoplane configuration with two floats.
1Its tailplane was supported by two booms and the floats, which extended well toward the rear of the aircraft.
2Its two 745-kilowatt (1,000-horsepower) Isotta-Fraschini engines were mounted in tandem, each driving a two-bladed propeller, one in the nose in a tractor configuration and the other at the rear of the fuselage in a pusher configuration.Vašiček, Aviation History, September 2002, p. 35.
3Operational historyThe S.65 was excluded from the 1929 race due to mechanical problems, and Italy was instead represented in the race by one Macchi M.52R and two Macchi M.67 seaplanes.Vašiček, Aviation History, September 2002, p. 35.
4Tomasso Dal Molin of the Italian Schneider Trophy racing team was killed flying the S.65 during training at Lake Garda in northern Italy in 1930.
